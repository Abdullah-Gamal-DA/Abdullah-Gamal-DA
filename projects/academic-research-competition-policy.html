Role of Competition Policy in Enhancing Food Security and Agricultural Productivity (1990–2023) — Case Study: Egypt

This study (Jan 2025 – Apr 2025) analyzed the role of competition policy in enhancing food security and agricultural productivity in Egypt between 1990 and 2023 using descriptive, graphical and econometric methods, including an ARDL model estimated in EViews 12. Results indicate competition policies implemented since 2018 did not have a significant direct effect on food security but contributed significantly to increasing agricultural output. The study found a positive relationship between agricultural output and food security in both the short and long run. Imports showed a positive short-run effect but a negative long-run effect due to supply-chain volatility. Causality tests confirmed an effect of competition policy on agricultural output. Recommendations include adopting comprehensive competition-enhancing policies, increasing investment, adopting modern technologies, improving labor efficiency, reducing import dependence, and five-year development plans to strengthen food security.
